# Supplier Portal Guide - User Guide

**Audience**: Suppliers, vendor sustainability managers, product carbon footprint data providers
**Prerequisites**: Portal invitation email from customer organization, product emission data or LCA reports
**Time**: 30-45 minutes for initial setup, 15-30 minutes per PCF submission

---

## Overview

Welcome to the GL-VCCI Supplier Portal! This guide is designed for suppliers who have been invited by their customers to submit Product Carbon Footprint (PCF) data. The portal makes it easy to share your product emission data using industry-standard formats, helping your customers meet their Scope 3 reporting requirements.

### What You'll Learn
- How to register and access the supplier portal
- Complete your supplier profile
- Submit PCF data in PACT Pathfinder format
- Understand data quality guidelines
- Track submission status and history
- Troubleshoot common issues

### Why Submit PCF Data?

**Benefits to Your Organization**:
- **Competitive Advantage**: More customers require PCF data for procurement decisions
- **Relationship Strengthening**: Demonstrate commitment to sustainability
- **Efficiency**: Submit once, share with multiple customers
- **Visibility**: Showcase emission reduction efforts
- **Future-Proofing**: Meet emerging regulatory requirements (ESRS, CSRD)

**Industry Context**:
- 70% of large enterprises now track Scope 3 emissions
- PCF data is becoming a standard procurement requirement
- EU CSRD requires value chain emission disclosure
- CDP and other frameworks mandate Scope 3 reporting

## Section 3: Understanding PCF Data Requirements

### PACT Pathfinder Format

The portal uses the **Partnership for Carbon Transparency (PACT) Pathfinder** standard - the industry-standard format for exchanging PCF data.

**Key Components**:
1. **Product Identification**: Name, ID, description
2. **Carbon Footprint Value**: Total emissions (kgCO2e per declared unit)
3. **Declared Unit**: Functional unit (e.g., 1 kg, 1 piece, 1 kWh)
4. **Lifecycle Stages**: Cradle-to-gate, cradle-to-grave, etc.
5. **Reference Period**: When data was collected
6. **Geographic Scope**: Where product is manufactured
7. **Data Quality**: Indicator of calculation accuracy
8. **Methodology**: Standards followed

**üí° Tip**: If you already have PCF data in another format (EPD, LCA report), the portal can help convert it to Pathfinder format.

### Data Quality Indicators

The platform uses a standardized data quality rating:

**Coverage (Completeness)**:
- **100%**: All processes included
- **95-99%**: Minor processes excluded (documented)
- **<95%**: Significant gaps in data

**Technological Correlation**:
- **1**: Primary data from actual production
- **2**: Secondary data from similar technology
- **3**: Generic industry data

**Temporal Correlation**:
- **1**: Data less than 3 years old
- **2**: Data 3-6 years old
- **3**: Data more than 6 years old

**Geographical Correlation**:
- **1**: Data from actual production location
- **2**: Data from same country
- **3**: Data from different region

**Overall Data Quality Rating** (DQR):
- **5 (Excellent)**: Primary data, recent, site-specific
- **4 (Good)**: Mix of primary and secondary, mostly current
- **3 (Fair)**: Industry averages, reasonably current
- **2 (Poor)**: Generic data, outdated
- **1 (Very Poor)**: Estimates, no verification

**üí° Tip**: Aim for DQR of 4 or higher. Primary data from your operations always beats generic estimates.

### Required vs. Optional Data

**Required for Submission**:
- ‚úÖ Product name and ID
- ‚úÖ Total carbon footprint value
- ‚úÖ Declared unit
- ‚úÖ Lifecycle stage boundaries
- ‚úÖ Reference period (data collection dates)
- ‚úÖ Data quality indicator

**Optional but Recommended**:
- üí° Biogenic carbon content (if applicable)
- üí° Carbon removals (if applicable)
- üí° Uncertainty range
- üí° Methodology documentation
- üí° Third-party verification status
- üí° Supporting documentation (LCA report, EPD)

**Highly Valued by Customers**:
- ‚≠ê Breakdown by lifecycle stage (materials, production, transport)
- ‚≠ê Allocation methodology for co-products
- ‚≠ê Key assumptions and exclusions
- ‚≠ê Improvement actions planned

   - **Product Image**: Upload photo (optional but helpful)

[Screenshot: Product identification form]

**Carbon Footprint Data**:

6. **Enter emission values**:
   - **Total Carbon Footprint**: Value in kgCO2e
   - **Declared Unit**: What the footprint relates to
     - Examples: "1 kg", "1 piece", "1 liter", "1 kWh"
   - **Functional Unit Description**: If complex unit (e.g., "per 1000 hours of operation")

7. **Specify lifecycle boundaries**:
   - **Select lifecycle stage**:
     - ‚òëÔ∏è Cradle-to-Gate (raw materials ‚Üí factory gate) - Most common
     - ‚òê Cradle-to-Grave (includes use phase and end-of-life)
     - ‚òê Gate-to-Gate (manufacturing only)
     - ‚òê Cradle-to-Cradle (full circular economy)

8. **Breakdown by lifecycle stage** (optional but valuable):
   - **A1-A3: Product Stage**
     - A1 Raw Material Supply: ___ kgCO2e
     - A2 Transport to Factory: ___ kgCO2e
     - A3 Manufacturing: ___ kgCO2e
   - **A4-A5: Construction/Installation** (if applicable)
   - **B1-B7: Use Stage** (if included)
   - **C1-C4: End of Life** (if included)

[Screenshot: Lifecycle breakdown form]

**Reference Period**:

9. **Specify data collection period**:
   - **Start Date**: When data collection began (e.g., 2024-01-01)
   - **End Date**: When data collection ended (e.g., 2024-12-31)
   - **Representative Period**: "Annual average 2024" or "Q4 2024 production"

**Geographic Information**:

10. **Enter location details**:
    - **Manufacturing Country**: Where product is made
    - **Manufacturing Region**: State/province (if relevant)
    - **Manufacturing Site**: Facility name or city
    - **Primary Material Sources**: Top 3 countries for raw materials

**Data Quality**:

11. **Assess and document data quality**:
    - **Data Quality Rating**: Select 1-5 based on criteria above
    - **Primary Data Percentage**: % of data from your operations
    - **Data Sources**:
      - ‚òëÔ∏è Internal production data
      - ‚òëÔ∏è Supplier-specific data
      - ‚òê Industry averages
      - ‚òê LCA databases (specify: Ecoinvent, GaBi, etc.)
    - **Explain quality rating**: Brief justification

[Screenshot: Data quality assessment form]

**Methodology**:

12. **Document calculation methodology**:
    - **Standards Followed**:
      - ‚òëÔ∏è ISO 14067 (Carbon footprint of products)
      - ‚òëÔ∏è ISO 14040/14044 (LCA)
      - ‚òê PAS 2050
      - ‚òê GHG Protocol Product Standard
      - ‚òëÔ∏è PACT Pathfinder
    - **LCA Software Used**: SimaPro, GaBi, OpenLCA, etc. (if applicable)
    - **Allocation Method**: If co-products exist
      - Physical allocation (mass, energy)
      - Economic allocation (revenue)
    - **Cut-off Criteria**: Processes excluded (e.g., <1% of total impact)

**Special Considerations**:

13. **Additional data** (if applicable):
    - **Biogenic Carbon**: ___ kgCO2e (carbon from biomass)
    - **Carbon Removals**: ___ kgCO2e (if product stores carbon)
    - **Fossil Carbon**: ___ kgCO2e (carbon from fossil fuels)
    - **Land Use Change**: ___ kgCO2e (if significant)
    - **Uncertainty Range**: ¬± __% or (__to__ kgCO2e)

**Verification**:

14. **Verification status**:
    - **Third-Party Verified**: Yes/No
    - **Verifier Organization**: Name of certifier
    - **Verification Standard**: ISO 14067, ISO 14064-3, etc.
    - **Verification Date**: When verified
    - **Certificate Number**: Reference number

**Supporting Documentation**:

15. **Upload supporting files** (optional but builds trust):
    - LCA report (PDF)

## Section 5: Data Quality Guidelines

### Best Practices for High-Quality PCF Data

**1. Use Primary Data Wherever Possible**
- ‚úÖ Measure actual energy consumption at your facilities
- ‚úÖ Use supplier-specific emission factors
- ‚úÖ Track actual material quantities
- ‚ùå Avoid generic industry averages when primary data available

**2. Document Your Methodology**
- ‚úÖ Clearly state what's included and excluded
- ‚úÖ Explain allocation methods for co-products
- ‚úÖ Reference standards followed
- ‚úÖ Specify database versions (e.g., Ecoinvent 3.9)

**3. Keep Data Current**
- ‚úÖ Update PCF data annually minimum
- ‚úÖ Note if significant process changes occurred
- ‚úÖ Use most recent emission factors
- ‚úÖ Reflect current product composition

**4. Be Transparent About Uncertainty**
- ‚úÖ Provide uncertainty ranges if available
- ‚úÖ Explain limitations in data
- ‚úÖ Note assumptions made
- ‚úÖ Identify areas for improvement

**5. Validate Before Submitting**
- ‚úÖ Cross-check calculations
- ‚úÖ Compare to similar products
- ‚úÖ Review for order-of-magnitude errors
- ‚úÖ Have colleague review

**üí° Tip**: Higher quality data leads to better customer relationships and more accurate supply chain emissions.

### Common Data Quality Issues

**Issue: Incomplete System Boundaries**
**Problem**: Excluding significant lifecycle stages
**Solution**: Clearly document what's included. If cradle-to-gate, ensure all upstream processes covered.

**Issue: Outdated Data**
**Problem**: Using emission factors or production data more than 5 years old
**Solution**: Update with current data. Note temporal applicability in submission.

**Issue: Inconsistent Units**
**Problem**: Mixing units (e.g., kg and lbs, kWh and MJ)
**Solution**: Standardize on metric units. Portal assumes kg, km, kWh unless specified.

**Issue: Missing Allocation Documentation**
**Problem**: Not explaining how emissions split between co-products
**Solution**: Document allocation basis (mass, energy, economic value) and calculations.

**Issue: Unrealistic Precision**
**Problem**: Reporting 7.3458291 kgCO2e when underlying data doesn't support that precision
**Solution**: Round to appropriate precision (typically 2-3 significant figures).

**Issue: Ignoring Uncertainty**
**Problem**: Presenting estimates as exact values
**Solution**: Provide uncertainty range or acknowledge data limitations.

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues and Solutions

**Issue**: Cannot access invitation link - "Link expired or invalid"
**Solution**:
1. Check email date - invitations expire after 30 days
2. Verify you're using complete URL (no line breaks)
3. Try different browser or clear browser cache
4. Contact customer to resend invitation
5. Check spam folder for original invitation

**Issue**: Forgot password
**Solution**:
1. Click "Forgot Password" on login page
2. Enter email address
3. Check email for reset link (check spam folder)
4. Reset link valid for 1 hour
5. Create new password
6. If no email received, contact support

**Issue**: Upload template validation errors - "Invalid date format"
**Solution**:
1. Dates must be in YYYY-MM-DD format (e.g., 2024-03-15)
2. Check Excel hasn't auto-converted dates to different format
3. Set column format to "Text" before entering dates
4. Download fresh template if continuing issues
5. Review example rows in template

**Issue**: Submission rejected - "Data quality insufficient"
**Solution**:
1. Review customer's minimum quality requirements
2. Check which quality criteria failed
3. Identify where to source better data:
   - Replace estimates with primary data
   - Use supplier-specific factors
   - Update outdated data sources
4. Document methodology improvements
5. Consider third-party verification
6. Contact customer to discuss if unable to meet requirements

**Issue**: Cannot find my product in customer's request
**Solution**:
1. Search using your internal product code
2. Search using customer's product code (if known)
3. Check product description for match
4. Contact customer to clarify which product requested
5. Use "Other/Not Listed" option if product not shown

**Issue**: Calculation seems incorrect - footprint too high or low
**Solution**:
1. Verify units are consistent (kg vs. lbs, km vs. miles)
2. Check declared unit matches your calculation
3. Ensure lifecycle boundary matches (cradle-to-gate vs. cradle-to-grave)
4. Verify emission factor sources and vintages
5. Compare to similar products for sanity check
6. Review allocation if co-products exist
7. Contact support if still uncertain

**Issue**: Customer not responding to submission
**Solution**:
1. Check submission status - may be in review queue
2. Typical review time is 3-5 business days
3. Send polite follow-up message via portal
4. Check if customer has questions in feedback section
5. Contact customer via email/phone if urgent
6. After 2 weeks, escalate to customer's admin

**Issue**: Need to update already-submitted data
**Solution**:
1. Cannot edit after submission (audit trail)
2. Options:
   - Withdraw submission and resubmit (before customer reviews)
   - Wait for customer feedback and submit revision
   - Submit new version with change notes
3. Contact customer to explain situation
4. For minor corrections, message customer rather than full resubmission

**Issue**: API integration failing - "Authentication error"
**Solution**:
1. Verify API key is correct (copy-paste to avoid typos)
2. Check key hasn't expired (typically 1-year validity)
3. Ensure Authorization header format: "Bearer YOUR_API_KEY"
4. Verify you're using production endpoint, not sandbox
5. Check API rate limits (100 requests/minute)
6. Review API logs for detailed error messages
7. Contact support with request ID for assistance

## FAQ

**Q: How long does it take to complete my first submission?**
**A**: First submission typically takes 30-45 minutes including registration. Subsequent submissions are faster (15-20 minutes) as you're familiar with the process. Bulk uploads can submit dozens of products in similar time.

**Q: What if I don't have a full LCA for my product?**
**A**: You have options:
1. Simplified PCF calculator (portal provides)
2. Estimate based on materials and energy
3. Use industry average with documentation
4. Commission LCA study (most accurate)
5. Engage LCA consultant for guidance
Note: Lower quality data is better than no data. Document limitations clearly.

**Q: Can I submit in units other than kgCO2e?**
**A**: Platform standardizes on kgCO2e (kilograms CO2 equivalent), which includes all greenhouse gases (CO2, CH4, N2O, etc.) converted to CO2 equivalents using GWP100. If you have data in other units:
- tCO2e (tonnes): Convert by dividing by 1000
- gCO2e (grams): Convert by multiplying by 1000
- lbsCO2e (pounds): Convert by multiplying by 0.453592

**Q: How often should I update my PCF data?**
**A**: Best practices:
- Annually: Minimum for most products
- When significant changes occur: New materials, process changes, energy mix changes
- When customer requests: Some industries require quarterly updates
- When emission factors update: Major database updates
Set up reminders to review annually even if no changes.

**Q: Is my data shared with other companies?**
**A**: No. Your PCF data is shared only with:
1. Customers you explicitly submit to
2. No other suppliers or competitors
3. Anonymized aggregates may be used for industry benchmarking (opt-in only)
You control who sees your data. Each customer relationship is isolated.

**Q: What if my customer uses a different PCF format?**
**A**: Portal uses PACT Pathfinder (industry standard), which is compatible with:
- ISO 14067
- GHG Protocol Product Standard
- WBCSD Guidance
- Most EPD formats
If customer needs different format, portal can export to:
- Excel (custom template)
- JSON (via API)
- EPD XML
Contact support for format conversion assistance.

**Q: Do I need to pay to use the supplier portal?**
**A**: No. Supplier portal access is free for invited suppliers. Your customer's organization pays for the platform. You only need:
- Valid invitation from customer
- Internet connection
- Modern web browser
No subscription or per-submission fees.

**Q: Can multiple people from my company access the portal?**
**A**: Yes. Invite colleagues:
1. Navigate to Profile > Team Members
2. Click "Invite Team Member"
3. Enter their email and role
4. They receive invitation to register
Roles available:
- Admin: Full access, manage team
- Contributor: Submit and edit data
- Viewer: Read-only access
Useful for collaboration between sustainability, engineering, and quality teams.

**Q: What happens if I miss the submission deadline?**
**A**: Deadlines are guidelines to help customers with their reporting timelines. If you miss a deadline:
1. Submit as soon as possible
2. Message customer explaining delay
3. Most customers will accept late submissions
4. May affect their reporting schedule
5. Future deadlines may be adjusted
Communicate proactively if you'll miss a deadline.

**Q: Can I see how my PCF data is used in my customer's reports?**
**A**: Some customers share reports back with suppliers, but this varies by organization. You can:
1. Request to see how your data was used
2. Ask for summary of their Scope 3 results
3. Some customers provide supplier-specific feedback
4. Transparency depends on customer policy
This is at customer's discretion for confidentiality reasons.
