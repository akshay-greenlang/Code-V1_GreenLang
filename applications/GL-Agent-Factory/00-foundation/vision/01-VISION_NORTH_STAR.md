## 1. Vision Statement

By 2030, every organization on Earth that needs to measure, report, reduce, or verify their climate impact will use GreenLang-generated agents to do so - accurately, affordably, and at scale.

## 6. Guiding Principles

### Principle 1: Factory First

Every architectural and product decision must optimize for factory-scale production, not one-off development.

**Implications:**
- Design for automation, not manual intervention
- Build reusable patterns, not bespoke solutions
- Invest in generation infrastructure over individual agents
- Measure velocity, not just quality

### Principle 2: Quality is Non-Negotiable

No agent ships without meeting all 12 quality dimensions. Quality is not a tradeoff - it is a prerequisite.

**Implications:**
- Block releases that fail quality gates
- Invest in validation infrastructure
- Automate quality checks completely
- Treat quality issues as critical bugs

### Principle 3: Zero-Hallucination or Nothing

Every number must be traceable to a deterministic calculation. Hallucinated numbers are production incidents.

**Implications:**
- Tool-first architecture is mandatory
- All calculations via base agents
- Complete provenance tracking
- Audit-ready by default

### Principle 4: Regulatory Compliance by Design

Compliance is not a feature - it is the foundation. Every agent is born compliant.

**Implications:**
- Build compliance into the factory, not after
- Track all regulatory frameworks
- Update rapidly when regulations change
- Document compliance automatically

### Principle 5: Customer Impact Over Technical Elegance

The factory exists to solve customer problems and enable climate action, not to demonstrate technical sophistication.

**Implications:**
- Prioritize agents that drive revenue
- Focus on real customer use cases
- Measure by adoption, not by code
- Ship fast, then iterate
