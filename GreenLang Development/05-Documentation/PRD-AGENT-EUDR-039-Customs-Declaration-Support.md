## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Problem Statement

The EU Deforestation Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2023/1115) Article 4(2) requires that operators presenting EUDR-regulated commodities or their derived products at EU customs must provide the Due Diligence Statement (DDS) reference number to customs authorities before goods may be released for free circulation, placed under customs procedures, or exported from the EU. Article 23 mandates that customs authorities shall check whether a valid DDS has been submitted before releasing goods, and Article 24 empowers customs authorities to suspend the release of, seize, or order the destruction of goods that do not comply with the EUDR. The Union Customs Code (Regulation (EU) No 952/2013) governs the overarching customs declaration framework, requiring operators to submit customs declarations using the Single Administrative Document (SAD) or electronic equivalents, with product classifications based on the 8-digit Combined Nomenclature (CN) codes that extend the 6-digit Harmonized System (HS) codes used in international trade.

The intersection of EUDR compliance and customs declaration creates a critical operational challenge for EU importers. Every shipment of cattle, cocoa, coffee, palm oil, rubber, soya, or wood -- and their hundreds of derived products -- must be correctly classified under CN codes that match the EUDR commodity scope defined in Annex I, linked to a valid DDS reference number generated by the EU Information System, declared with accurate country of origin that matches the supply chain geolocation data, valued correctly for customs purposes using CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight) or FOB (Free on Board) methodologies, and submitted through EU customs systems including the New Computerised Transit System (NCTS), the Automated Import System (AIS), and the Import Control System 2 (ICS2). A single error in any of these steps can result in goods being held at the border, seized by customs authorities, or destroyed -- causing immediate financial loss and supply chain disruption.

Real-world operators face the following critical gaps at the customs-EUDR intersection:

- **No automated CN code mapping for EUDR commodities**: The 7 EUDR-regulated commodities span over 200 CN codes across Chapters 01, 02, 04, 09, 12, 15, 18, 23, 38, 40, 41, 44, 47, 48, and 94 of the Combined Nomenclature. Operators manually look up CN codes, leading to misclassification errors that trigger customs rejections, duty miscalculations, and EUDR compliance failures. There is no engine that automatically maps EUDR commodity categories and their derived products to the correct 8-digit CN codes.

- **No HS code validation for global trade partners**: Operators import from non-EU countries that use 6-digit HS codes, 10-digit national tariff codes, or local classification systems. There is no validator that cross-references supplier HS codes against the EU Combined Nomenclature to ensure consistency and identify potential misclassifications before goods reach the EU border.

- **No automated customs declaration form generation**: The Single Administrative Document (SAD) contains 54 boxes requiring specific data elements including CN codes, country of origin, customs value, transport mode, and now EUDR DDS reference numbers. Operators manually fill SAD forms or rely on customs brokers who may not understand EUDR requirements. There is no generator that automatically produces SAD forms pre-populated with EUDR-compliant data from upstream agents.

- **No country of origin cross-validation against supply chain data**: Customs declarations require a country of origin declaration, but operators may declare a different country than what their supply chain traceability data shows. There is no validator that cross-references the customs declaration country of origin against the geolocation data from EUDR-001 supply chain mapping, EUDR-002 geolocation verification, and EUDR-007 GPS coordinate validation to detect discrepancies before submission.

- **No automated customs value calculation for EUDR commodities**: Customs value determines the duty amount and must be calculated using WTO Valuation Agreement methods (transaction value, deductive value, computed value, or fallback). EUDR commodities have commodity-specific valuation considerations including plantation-gate prices, FOB port prices, CIF EU port prices, and processing value additions. There is no calculator that automatically determines customs value from commercial invoices, freight documents, and insurance certificates.

- **No DDS-to-customs-declaration linkage**: EUDR Article 4(2) requires the DDS reference number to be presented to customs authorities, but there is no mechanism to automatically embed the DDS reference number into the customs declaration, verify that the DDS is valid and not expired before customs submission, or track the linkage between customs declarations and their associated DDS documents for audit purposes.

- **No tariff and duty calculation for EUDR commodities**: EU import duties vary by CN code, country of origin, trade agreement applicability (GSP, GSP+, EBA, bilateral FTAs), and anti-dumping or countervailing duty provisions. Operators manually calculate duties or rely on customs brokers, leading to overpayment or underpayment that triggers customs audits. There is no calculator that determines the applicable tariff rate and duty amount for EUDR-regulated commodities based on their CN code, origin, and applicable trade preferences.

- **No customs authority system interface**: EU customs declarations must be submitted electronically through national customs systems that connect to the EU-wide NCTS (transit), AIS (import), and ICS2 (pre-arrival). Each Member State operates its own customs IT platform (ATLAS in Germany, DELTA in France, AIDA in Italy). There is no agent that formats and transmits customs declarations to these systems while ensuring EUDR compliance data is included.

- **No pre-submission EUDR compliance verification**: Before customs clearance, customs authorities verify EUDR compliance by checking DDS validity, product classification, and risk status. Operators have no way to pre-verify their customs declarations against EUDR requirements before submitting to customs, leading to border rejections, delays, and penalties.

Without solving these problems, EU operators face goods held at the border for days or weeks while customs authorities verify EUDR compliance, seizure and destruction of non-compliant goods under Article 24, penalties of up to 4% of annual EU turnover under Article 25, supply chain disruption affecting downstream manufacturing and retail, additional customs duties and interest charges for valuation errors, and reputational damage from public naming of non-compliant operators.

### 1.2 Solution Overview

Agent-EUDR-039: Customs Declaration Support is the specialized customs-EUDR bridge agent that automates the generation, validation, and submission of customs declarations for EUDR-regulated commodities. It sits between the EUDR reporting pipeline (EUDR-037 DDS Creator, EUDR-038 Reference Number Generator) and EU customs systems (NCTS, AIS, ICS2), ensuring that every customs declaration for EUDR-regulated goods contains correct CN/HS codes, valid DDS reference numbers, verified country of origin data, accurate customs valuations, and applicable tariff calculations. It is the 39th agent in the EUDR agent family and the fourth agent in the Reporting sub-category (Category 6).

Core capabilities:

1. **CN Code Mapper** -- Maps all 7 EUDR-regulated commodities and their derived products to the correct 8-digit Combined Nomenclature codes using a comprehensive lookup engine covering 200+ CN codes across 15 CN chapters. Handles commodity-specific classification rules, derived product hierarchies, and CN code version updates. Supports reverse lookup from CN code to EUDR commodity category for incoming customs data reconciliation.

2. **HS Code Validator** -- Validates 6-digit Harmonized System codes provided by non-EU suppliers against the EU Combined Nomenclature, detects misclassifications, resolves HS-to-CN code mapping with country-specific national tariff line extensions, and provides classification confidence scores. Supports all WCO HS versions (2017, 2022, 2027) with version transition handling.

3. **Declaration Form Generator** -- Generates customs declaration forms in electronic format conforming to the Single Administrative Document (SAD) specification, EU Customs Data Model (EUCDM), and UCC Delegated Act Annex B data requirements. Pre-populates all 54 SAD boxes from upstream EUDR agent data including product descriptions, CN codes, country of origin, customs values, DDS reference numbers, and transport details. Supports both import (IM) and export (EX) declaration types.

4. **Country of Origin Validator** -- Cross-validates the declared country of origin on customs declarations against geolocation data from EUDR-001 supply chain mapping, EUDR-002 geolocation verification, EUDR-006 plot boundary management, and EUDR-007 GPS coordinate validation. Detects origin declaration discrepancies, identifies potential circumvention (goods routed through third countries), and validates preferential origin claims against rules of origin criteria.

5. **Quantity and Value Calculator** -- Calculates customs values for EUDR-regulated commodity shipments using WTO Valuation Agreement methods. Determines transaction value from commercial invoices, adds CIF adjustments (freight, insurance, loading charges), calculates FOB values for export declarations, handles currency conversion using ECB reference rates, and computes statistical values for Intrastat reporting.

6. **DDS Integration Module** -- Links customs declarations to Due Diligence Statements by embedding DDS reference numbers from EUDR-038, verifying DDS validity and status before customs submission, tracking the customs-DDS association for audit purposes, and ensuring that DDS scope (commodity, quantity, origin) matches the customs declaration scope. Prevents submission of customs declarations for goods without valid DDS.

7. **Tariff Calculator** -- Calculates applicable import duties, VAT, excise duties, and other charges for EUDR-regulated commodities based on CN code, country of origin, customs value, and applicable trade preferences. Implements EU Common Customs Tariff (CCT) rates, preferential tariff rates under GSP/GSP+/EBA/bilateral FTAs, anti-dumping duties, countervailing duties, safeguard measures, and tariff rate quotas (TRQs) for agricultural commodities.

8. **Customs Authority Interface** -- Formats and transmits customs declarations to EU customs IT systems. Supports the EU Customs Single Window Environment for Certificates (EU-SWE-C), NCTS Phase 5 for transit declarations, AIS for import declarations, and ICS2 for pre-arrival Entry Summary Declarations (ENS). Implements the EU Customs Data Model (EUCDM) message formats and handles response processing including MRN (Movement Reference Number) assignment.

9. **Compliance Checker** -- Performs comprehensive pre-submission verification of customs declarations against EUDR Article 4 requirements, Article 23 customs control criteria, and Article 24 suspension and seizure triggers. Validates DDS reference number presence and validity, CN code EUDR scope coverage, country of origin consistency with supply chain data, quantity reconciliation with DDS declarations, and generates a compliance readiness score with pass/fail determination before customs submission.

- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)**: 400,000+ operators and traders affected by EUDR across the EU must submit customs declarations for every regulated commodity entering or leaving the EU market. The customs compliance technology market for EUDR-regulated commodities is estimated at 1-3 billion EUR, as operators need automated customs declaration tools that integrate EUDR compliance requirements with existing Union Customs Code obligations. Each operator may process dozens to thousands of customs declarations per year.
- **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**: 100,000+ EU importers of the 7 regulated commodities requiring integrated EUDR-customs declaration tools. These operators collectively process millions of customs declarations annually for EUDR-scope goods. Estimated market of 400M-800M EUR.
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**: Target 500+ enterprise customers in Year 1, representing 20-40M EUR in customs declaration module ARR. Enterprise customers are the primary target because they handle the highest declaration volumes and face the greatest CN code complexity across multiple commodity categories.

### 2.4 Differentiation Strategy

1. **EUDR-customs bridge architecture** -- Not a standalone customs tool or a standalone EUDR tool. Purpose-built to bridge the gap between EUDR compliance (38 upstream agents) and EU customs systems (NCTS, AIS, ICS2). No competitor offers this integrated bridge.
2. **200+ EUDR CN code mapping engine** -- Comprehensive, auditable mapping of all 7 EUDR commodities and their derived products to 8-digit CN codes, covering cattle (CN 0102, 0201-0202, 4101-4103), cocoa (CN 1801-1806), coffee (CN 0901), palm oil (CN 1511-1514), rubber (CN 4001), soya (CN 1201, 2304), and wood (CN 44, 47, 48, 94).
3. **Supply chain origin cross-validation** -- Unique capability to cross-reference customs declaration country of origin against upstream geolocation data from 7 traceability agents. No competitor validates origin declarations against actual supply chain data.
4. **Zero-hallucination calculations** -- All CN code mapping, customs value calculation, tariff determination, and compliance checking is deterministic. No LLM in the critical path. Same inputs produce bit-identical outputs. Complete SHA-256 provenance chain.
5. **DDS auto-linkage** -- Automatically embeds DDS reference numbers from EUDR-038, verifies DDS validity, and ensures scope alignment between DDS and customs declaration. No manual DDS reference entry required.
6. **Pre-submission compliance gate** -- Comprehensive EUDR Article 4/23/24 compliance verification before customs submission, preventing border rejections and seizures.

**Duty Calculation Formula:**
```
Total Charges (EUR) = Import Duty + Anti-Dumping Duty + Countervailing Duty
                    + Safeguard Duty + VAT + Excise Duty

Where:
- Import Duty = Customs Value x Applicable Tariff Rate
  (CCT rate if MFN; preferential rate if FTA/GSP applies)
- Anti-Dumping Duty = Customs Value x ADD Rate (or specific amount per unit)
- Countervailing Duty = Customs Value x CVD Rate
- Safeguard Duty = Customs Value x Safeguard Rate
- VAT = (Customs Value + Import Duty + ADD + CVD + Safeguard) x Member State VAT Rate
- Excise Duty = Per-unit excise amount (if applicable)
```

```
I want to submit customs declarations electronically to EU customs systems from a single interface,
So that I can file declarations across multiple Member States without switching between different national customs IT systems.
```

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Formats customs declarations in EU Customs Data Model (EUCDM) message format for electronic submission
- [ ] Supports AIS (Automated Import System) for import declarations in all 27 EU Member States
- [ ] Supports NCTS Phase 5 (New Computerised Transit System) for transit declarations
- [ ] Supports ICS2 Release 3 (Import Control System 2) for Entry Summary Declarations (ENS)
- [ ] Supports EU-SWE-C (Single Window Environment for Customs -- Certificates) for certificate exchange including EUDR DDS references
- [ ] Handles Member State-specific customs IT systems: ATLAS (Germany), DELTA-G/DELTA-X (France), AIDA (Italy), AGS (Netherlands), DELT@ (Belgium), DV1 (Spain)
- [ ] Processes customs authority response messages: acceptance (MRN assignment), rejection (error codes), query (additional information request), release (goods cleared), suspension (goods held)
- [ ] Assigns and tracks Movement Reference Number (MRN) for each accepted declaration
- [ ] Implements retry logic for transient communication failures (exponential backoff, max 3 retries)
- [ ] Supports asynchronous submission with webhook callbacks for response notification
- [ ] Logs all customs system communications with timestamps and message content
- [ ] Maintains secure TLS 1.3 connections to customs authority endpoints

**Customs System Message Flows:**

| System | Message Type | Direction | Format | EUDR Data Included |
|--------|-------------|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| AIS | Import Declaration (IM) | Operator -> Customs | EUCDM XML | DDS ref in Box 44, CN codes, origin |
| AIS | MRN Assignment | Customs -> Operator | EUCDM XML | Declaration accepted, MRN assigned |
| AIS | Release Notification | Customs -> Operator | EUCDM XML | Goods released for free circulation |
| AIS | Suspension Notice | Customs -> Operator | EUCDM XML | EUDR compliance check pending |
| NCTS | Transit Declaration (T1/T2) | Operator -> Customs | EUCDM XML | DDS ref, CN codes, transit route |
| ICS2 | Entry Summary Declaration (ENS) | Carrier -> Customs | EUCDM XML | Pre-arrival EUDR commodity data |
| EU-SWE-C | Certificate Exchange | Operator <-> Customs | EU-SWE XML | DDS certificate reference |

**Non-Functional Requirements:**

- Reliability: 99.5% submission success rate (excluding customs authority downtime)
- Retry: Exponential backoff (1s, 5s, 30s) with max 3 retries for transient failures

#### Feature 9: Compliance Checker

**User Story:**
```
As a customs compliance manager,
I want comprehensive pre-submission verification of my customs declarations against EUDR requirements,
So that I can prevent border rejections, seizures, and penalties by catching compliance issues before submission.
```

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- [ ] Verifies DDS reference number is present in customs declaration (Art. 4(2) mandatory)
- [ ] Verifies DDS reference number is valid and not expired (via EUDR-038 verification API)
- [ ] Verifies CN code falls within EUDR Annex I commodity scope
- [ ] Verifies CN code matches DDS commodity category
- [ ] Verifies country of origin is consistent with DDS geolocation data
- [ ] Verifies country of origin risk classification (Art. 29) is addressed in DDS risk assessment
- [ ] Verifies declared quantity does not exceed DDS declared quantity
- [ ] Verifies declarant/operator matches DDS operator
- [ ] Verifies customs value is within acceptable range for the commodity and origin
- [ ] Verifies tariff calculation uses correct rate for CN code and origin combination
- [ ] Checks for known Article 24 seizure triggers: missing DDS, invalid DDS, origin discrepancy, quantity mismatch
- [ ] Generates compliance readiness score (0-100) with pass/fail determination
- [ ] Generates detailed compliance report with check-by-check results, severity levels, and remediation guidance
- [ ] Applies configurable compliance thresholds: "block" (score < 60), "warn" (score 60-85), "pass" (score > 85)
- [ ] Supports batch compliance checking for multiple declarations
- [ ] Logs every compliance check with provenance hash

**Compliance Check Matrix:**

| Check ID | Check Description | EUDR Article | Severity | Auto-Remediation |
|----------|------------------|-------------|----------|-----------------|
| CC-01 | DDS reference number present | Art. 4(2) | Critical | Block submission; redirect to EUDR-038 |
| CC-02 | DDS reference number valid | Art. 23(3) | Critical | Block submission; display DDS status |
| CC-03 | CN code in EUDR scope | Art. 1, Annex I | Critical | Block submission; trigger CN Code Mapper |
| CC-04 | CN code matches DDS commodity | Art. 4(2) | Critical | Block submission; flag mismatch |
| CC-05 | Origin matches DDS geolocation | Art. 9(1)(c) | Critical | Block submission; display discrepancy |
| CC-06 | Quantity within DDS scope | Art. 9(1)(b) | High | Warn; allow with justification |
| CC-07 | Operator matches DDS operator | Art. 4(1) | Critical | Block submission; verify delegation |
| CC-08 | Country risk addressed | Art. 29 | High | Warn; link to risk assessment |
| CC-09 | Customs value in range | UCC Art. 70 | Medium | Warn; flag for manual review |
| CC-10 | Tariff rate correct | UCC Art. 56 | Medium | Warn; recalculate |
| CC-11 | Declaration format valid | UCC Art. 162 | High | Block submission; list format errors |
| CC-12 | Transport documents present | UCC Art. 163 | Medium | Warn; list missing documents |

**Compliance Readiness Score Calculation:**
```
Compliance Score = Sum(Check Weight x Check Result) / Sum(Check Weights) x 100

Where:
- Critical checks: Weight = 20, Result = 1.0 (pass) or 0.0 (fail)
- High checks: Weight = 10, Result = 1.0 (pass) or 0.0 (fail)
- Medium checks: Weight = 5, Result = 1.0 (pass) or 0.0 (fail)

Pass Threshold: Score >= 85 (all Critical checks must pass)
Warn Threshold: Score 60-84 (some High/Medium checks failed)
Block Threshold: Score < 60 (Critical checks failed)
```

**Non-Functional Requirements:**
- Performance: Single compliance check < 3 seconds p95 (includes upstream agent queries)
- Accuracy: 100% of Article 24 seizure triggers detected (zero false negatives)
- False Positive Rate: < 3% false positive rate for compliance warnings
- Determinism: Same inputs produce identical compliance score
- Auditability: Complete check-by-check audit trail with provenance hashes

**Dependencies:**
- Feature 1: CN Code Mapper (EUDR scope verification)
- Feature 4: Country of Origin Validator (origin consistency)
- Feature 5: Quantity and Value Calculator (value range validation)
- Feature 6: DDS Integration Module (DDS linkage verification)
- Feature 7: Tariff Calculator (tariff rate verification)
- EUDR-038 Reference Number Generator (DDS reference validation)
- EUDR-016 Country Risk Evaluator (country risk classification)

**Estimated Effort:** 3 weeks (1 senior backend engineer, 1 compliance specialist)

**Edge Cases:**
- DDS pending EU IS acknowledgement -> Allow submission with "conditional pass" status; monitor DDS acknowledgement
- Multiple DDS references for blended product -> Verify each DDS covers its respective commodity portion
- Customs declaration for re-export of previously imported EUDR goods -> Verify original import DDS; apply re-export rules
- Emergency shipment with time pressure -> Provide "express check" mode with Critical checks only; flag remaining checks for post-clearance audit

```python
class CNCodeRecord(BaseModel):
    cn_code: str                         # 8-digit Combined Nomenclature code (e.g., "1801 00 00")
    cn_chapter: int                      # Chapter number (e.g., 18)
    cn_heading: int                      # Heading (e.g., 1801)
    cn_subheading: str                   # Full subheading
    description_en: str                  # English description
    eudr_commodity: EUDRCommodityCategory # EUDR commodity category
    eudr_scope: bool                     # True if within EUDR Annex I scope
    processing_level: ProcessingLevel    # RAW, SEMI_PROCESSED, PROCESSED, FINISHED
    unit_of_measure: str                 # kg, m3, units, etc.
    supplementary_unit: Optional[str]    # Additional unit if required
    cn_version: str                      # CN year version (e.g., "2026")
    valid_from: date                     # CN code validity start
    valid_to: Optional[date]             # CN code validity end (None if current)

# HS Code Validation Result
class HSCodeValidationResult(BaseModel):
    hs_code: str                         # 6-digit HS code validated
    hs_version: str                      # HS 2017, HS 2022, HS 2027
    is_valid: bool                       # Format and existence validation
    cn_code_mappings: List[CNCodeRecord] # Possible 8-digit CN codes
    eudr_scope: bool                     # Any mapped CN code in EUDR scope
    confidence_score: float              # 0-100 confidence in mapping
    warnings: List[str]                  # Classification warnings

class CustomsDeclaration(BaseModel):
    declaration_id: str                  # UUID
    declaration_type: DeclarationType    # IMPORT, EXPORT, TRANSIT, ENS

    currency: str                        # Invoice currency code
    exchange_rate: Decimal               # ECB rate to EUR
    dds_references: List[DDSReference]   # Linked DDS reference numbers

    compliance_score: float              # Pre-submission compliance score (0-100)
    compliance_status: ComplianceStatus  # PASS, WARN, BLOCK
    mrn: Optional[str]                   # Movement Reference Number (assigned by customs)
    submission_status: SubmissionStatus  # DRAFT, VALIDATED, SUBMITTED, ACCEPTED, REJECTED, RELEASED, SUSPENDED

    created_at: datetime
    submitted_at: Optional[datetime]
    released_at: Optional[datetime]
```

## 8. User Experience

### 8.1 User Flows

#### Flow 1: Import Declaration with EUDR Compliance (Customs Compliance Manager)

```
1. Customs compliance manager logs in to GL-EUDR-APP
2. Navigates to "Customs Declarations" module
3. Clicks "New Import Declaration" -> selects commodity (e.g., Cocoa from Ghana)
4. System auto-populates from upstream agents:
   - Product description from EUDR-001 supply chain data
   - CN code 1801 00 00 (cocoa beans) from CN Code Mapper
   - Country of origin "GH" from supply chain geolocation
   - Quantity 25,000 kg from EUDR-011 mass balance
   - Customs value EUR 62,500 from Value Calculator (CIF Rotterdam)
5. System auto-links DDS reference: DE-2026-OPR00123-000458-L
6. System runs Compliance Checker:
   - CC-01 DDS present: PASS
   - CC-02 DDS valid: PASS
   - CC-03 CN code EUDR scope: PASS (cocoa)
   - CC-04 CN code matches DDS: PASS
   - CC-05 Origin matches DDS: PASS (GH = Ghana)
   - CC-06 Quantity within DDS: PASS (25,000 <= 30,000 DDS qty)
   - Compliance Score: 98/100 -> PASS
7. System calculates tariff: 0% MFN for cocoa beans -> EUR 0 duty
8. Manager reviews declaration and clicks "Submit to Customs"
9. System submits to ATLAS (Germany AIS) via Customs Authority Interface
10. ATLAS returns MRN: 26DE1234567890 -> Declaration accepted
11. Goods released for free circulation at Hamburg port
```

#### Flow 2: Bulk Declaration Processing (Customs Broker)

```
1. Customs broker logs in to GL-EUDR-APP
2. Navigates to "Batch Declarations" module
3. Uploads CSV with 50 shipments for 5 different clients
4. System processes each shipment:
   a. Maps product descriptions to CN codes using CN Code Mapper
   b. Validates supplier HS codes using HS Code Validator
   c. Cross-validates country of origin using Origin Validator
   d. Calculates customs values using Value Calculator
   e. Links DDS references using DDS Integration Module
   f. Calculates tariffs using Tariff Calculator
   g. Runs compliance checks using Compliance Checker
5. System generates declaration preview dashboard:
   - 48 declarations: PASS (compliance score > 85)
   - 1 declaration: WARN (origin discrepancy, score 72)
   - 1 declaration: BLOCK (missing DDS, score 35)
6. Broker reviews and resolves warnings:
   - Origin discrepancy: confirms transshipment via approved route
   - Missing DDS: contacts client to generate DDS via EUDR-037
7. Once all resolved, broker clicks "Submit All"
8. System submits to appropriate customs systems per member state
9. Broker monitors submission status dashboard for MRN assignment
```

#### Flow 3: Pre-Arrival EUDR Verification (Freight Forwarder)

```
1. Freight forwarder receives advance shipping notice for rubber shipment
2. Logs in to GL-EUDR-APP before vessel arrives at Rotterdam
3. Enters shipment details:
   - CN code: 4001 22 00 (TSNR rubber)
   - Origin: Indonesia
   - Quantity: 40,000 kg
   - DDS reference: NL-2026-NL9876-000345-7
4. System runs pre-arrival compliance check:
   - DDS valid: PASS
   - CN code EUDR scope: PASS (rubber)
   - Origin: WARN (Indonesia is standard-risk country)
   - Quantity: PASS
   - Country risk: Standard risk -> Enhanced due diligence applied in DDS
5. System generates ICS2 Entry Summary Declaration for pre-arrival notification
6. System submits ENS to AGS (Netherlands ICS2)
7. AGS acknowledges ENS with MRN
8. Vessel arrives; goods are pre-cleared based on ENS + DDS compliance
9. Customs releases goods within 4 hours (vs. 48 hours without pre-clearance)
```

### 13.2 Golden Test Commodities

Each of the 7 EUDR commodities will have dedicated golden test scenarios:

1. **Correct classification**: Product correctly mapped to CN code, DDS linked, origin validated, tariff calculated -> Declaration PASS
2. **CN code misclassification**: Incorrect CN code detected and corrected by mapper -> Classification warning
3. **Origin discrepancy**: Declared origin differs from supply chain data -> Compliance BLOCK
4. **Missing DDS**: No valid DDS linked to declaration -> Compliance BLOCK
5. **DDS scope mismatch**: DDS commodity does not match CN code EUDR scope -> Compliance BLOCK
6. **Quantity exceedance**: Declaration quantity exceeds DDS declared quantity -> Compliance WARN
7. **Preferential tariff**: Eligible for trade preference; correct reduced duty applied -> Tariff optimization
8. **Anti-dumping duty**: Product from country with active anti-dumping measures -> ADD applied
9. **Customs value benchmark**: Invoice value significantly below market benchmark -> Valuation WARNING
10. **Customs system rejection**: Declaration format error detected and corrected -> Re-submission success

Total: 7 commodities x 10 scenarios = 70 golden test scenarios
