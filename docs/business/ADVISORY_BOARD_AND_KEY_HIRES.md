## PART 1: ADVISORY BOARD STRATEGY

### Purpose of Advisory Board

An exceptional advisory board provides:
1. **Strategic Guidance:** Navigate market dynamics, competition, product decisions
2. **Domain Expertise:** Climate science, regulatory affairs, enterprise software, fundraising
3. **Credibility:** Enhance brand with customers, investors, partners, and talent
4. **Network Access:** Introductions to customers, investors, strategic partners, and key hires
5. **Crisis Management:** Support during challenging periods (market shifts, competitive threats)

## ADVISORY BOARD ENGAGEMENT & GOVERNANCE

### Meeting Cadence

**Quarterly Advisory Board Meetings:**
- Format: Virtual or in-person (2-3 hours)
- Agenda:
  - Company performance update (ARR, customers, product, team)
  - Strategic challenges and key decisions
  - Advisor Q&A and recommendations
  - Action items and next steps
- Output: Meeting notes distributed within 48 hours, action items tracked

**Annual Advisory Summit:**
- Format: In-person full-day meeting (8 hours)
- Timing: Q4 of each year (strategic planning for next year)
- Agenda:
  - Year in review (successes, failures, lessons learned)
  - Market trends and competitive landscape
  - Annual strategic priorities for next year
  - Networking and relationship building among advisors
- Output: Annual strategic plan, advisory board feedback report

**Ad-Hoc Engagements:**
- Customer/investor intros: As needed, typically 2-4x per quarter
- Crisis advisory: As needed (competitive threats, regulatory changes, fundraising challenges)
- Deep dives: 2-3x per year on specific topics (e.g., international expansion, M&A)

---

### Advisor Communication Channels

**Advisor Slack Channel:**
- Private Slack channel for all advisors
- Used for quick questions, updates, intros
- Response expectation: Within 2-3 business days

**Monthly Email Update:**
- CEO sends monthly update to all advisors (even in non-meeting months)
- Format: Brief (1-2 pages), key metrics, highlights, asks
- Keeps advisors engaged and informed

**Quarterly Advisor Reports:**
- Formal quarterly report (deck) distributed 1 week before quarterly meeting
- Includes: financials, metrics, product roadmap, strategic initiatives, asks

### Advisor Contributions Tracking

**Maintain "Advisor Value Ledger":**
- Track contributions from each advisor:
  - Customer intros and conversion rate
  - Investor intros and fundraising outcomes

- Annual review: Assess advisor value and engagement
- Non-performing advisors: Have honest conversations, potentially reduce equity vesting if zero contribution

**Success Metrics for Advisors:**
- Customer intros: 5-10 per year (qualified enterprise leads)
- Investor intros: 3-5 per fundraise (top-tier VCs)
- Strategic guidance: Participate in 80%+ of quarterly meetings
- Recruiting: 1-2 executive candidate intros per year
- Thought leadership: 1-2 co-authored pieces or speaking engagements per year

## PART 2: KEY HIRE PROFILES (TOP 12 HIRES)

### HIRE #1: VP ENGINEERING (HIGHEST PRIORITY)

**Hire Timeline:** January 2026 (Q1 2026, Month 1-2)
**Compensation:** $200-250K base + 1.5-2.5% equity
**Reports To:** CTO/Co-Founder

#### Must-Have Qualifications
1. **Experience:** 15+ years software engineering, 5+ years VP/Director level
2. **Scale:** Scaled engineering team from 5-10 → 50-100+ people
3. **Platform:** Built developer platforms, APIs, or infrastructure (not just applications)
4. **B2B SaaS:** Enterprise SaaS experience ($10M-$100M+ ARR)
5. **Technical Depth:** Can review code, make architecture decisions, mentor senior engineers
6. **Hiring:** Has hired 30+ engineers (knows how to source, interview, close)
7. **Process:** Established engineering best practices (CI/CD, testing, code review, agile)
8. **Culture:** Built high-performing engineering cultures (low attrition, high velocity)

#### Nice-to-Have Qualifications
- Climate tech or sustainability domain experience
- European enterprise software experience (GDPR, EU regulations)
- Open source project leadership or contributions
- Developer tools or API platform experience (Stripe, Twilio, Segment, etc.)
- Has taken engineering org through hypergrowth (3x-5x in 18 months)
- Previous startup experience (early-stage VP Eng at successful startup)

#### Interview Process (6-8 Weeks)
1. **Phone Screen (45 min):** Recruiter or CEO
   - Experience overview, motivations, compensation expectations
   - Pass/Fail: Proceed to first round

2. **First Round (90 min):** CTO/Co-Founder
   - Technical depth: System design, architecture decisions, trade-offs
   - Leadership: Scaling teams, hiring, processes
   - Culture fit: Values alignment, communication style
   - Pass/Fail: Proceed to finalist round

3. **Finalist Round (Half-day, on-site or virtual):**
   - **Session 1 (60 min):** CEO - Strategy, vision, culture
   - **Session 2 (90 min):** Engineering team (all 3 engineers) - Technical deep dive, whiteboarding
   - **Session 3 (45 min):** Product Manager (if hired) - Engineering/Product collaboration
   - **Session 4 (30 min):** Advisor or Board Member - External validation

4. **Reference Checks:**
   - 3-5 references (must include 2-3 former direct reports)
   - Questions: Strengths, weaknesses, management style, how to work with them

5. **Offer:**
   - Extend offer within 48 hours of final interview
   - Negotiate quickly (VP Eng candidates often have multiple offers)
   - Close within 1 week of offer (do not let drag on)

#### Red Flags (Auto-Reject)
- Has not managed 10+ engineers (not enough scale experience)
- Only application development experience (no platform/infrastructure)
- High attrition on previous teams (multiple direct reports left within 1 year)
- Cannot code or review code (needs technical depth)
- Poor communication skills (cannot explain complex topics simply)
- Weak references or references refuse to give strong recommendation

#### Key Interview Questions
1. "Tell me about a time you scaled an engineering team from X to Y people. What worked? What didn't?"
2. "How do you balance velocity (shipping fast) with quality (low tech debt)?"
3. "Walk me through your hiring process. How do you source, interview, and close top engineers?"
4. "What engineering metrics do you track? How do you know if your team is performing well?"
5. "Tell me about a time you made a critical architecture decision. How did you approach it?"
6. "How do you build an inclusive and high-performing engineering culture?"
7. "What would you do in your first 30/60/90 days at GreenLang?"

#### Success Metrics (First Year)
- Scale engineering team from 3 → 12 people (4x growth)
- Ship v1.0 platform on time (Q2 2026 target)
- Establish engineering standards: CI/CD pipeline, automated testing (80%+ coverage), code review process, documentation standards
- Reduce bug rate by 30%+ through better testing and processes
- Engineering retention: 90%+ (lose no more than 1 engineer)
- Engineering velocity: 2x increase in story points or features shipped per quarter
- Hire quality: 100% of hires meet or exceed expectations after 6 months
