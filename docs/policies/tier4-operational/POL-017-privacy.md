## 1. Purpose

This Privacy Policy explains how GreenLang ("Company," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal data in connection with our Climate OS platform and related services. This policy fulfills our transparency obligations under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and other applicable data protection laws.

GreenLang is committed to protecting the privacy and security of personal data while enabling organizations to achieve their climate and sustainability goals through our carbon accounting, CBAM compliance, and environmental reporting solutions.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Users:** Individuals who access or use GreenLang products and services
- **Customers:** Organizations that subscribe to GreenLang services
- **Website Visitors:** Individuals who visit greenlang.io or related domains
- **Job Applicants:** Individuals who apply for employment at GreenLang
- **Business Contacts:** Individuals we interact with in a business capacity

This policy covers personal data processed through:
- GreenLang Climate OS platform
- CBAM Agent and compliance tools
- Customer portals and dashboards
- Marketing websites and communications
- Support channels and communications
- Recruitment processes

---

## 3. Policy Statement

GreenLang processes personal data lawfully, fairly, and transparently. We collect only the data necessary for specified purposes, maintain its accuracy, retain it only as long as needed, and protect it with appropriate security measures. We respect data subject rights and cooperate with supervisory authorities.

### 5.2 Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR Article 6)

| Legal Basis | Application |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Contract (Art. 6(1)(b))** | Processing necessary to perform our service agreement with you, including account creation, service delivery, billing, and support |
| **Legitimate Interest (Art. 6(1)(f))** | Processing necessary for our legitimate business interests, including security, fraud prevention, service improvement, and analytics, balanced against your rights |
| **Legal Obligation (Art. 6(1)(c))** | Processing required by law, including tax obligations, regulatory compliance, and responding to lawful requests |
| **Consent (Art. 6(1)(a))** | Processing based on your freely given consent, including marketing communications and optional cookies |

### 5.3 Legitimate Interest Assessment

For processing based on legitimate interest, we conduct balancing tests considering:
- Necessity of processing for the stated purpose
- Impact on data subjects' rights and freedoms
- Reasonable expectations of data subjects
- Safeguards implemented to protect data subjects

## 6. Data Subject Rights

Under GDPR and applicable laws, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

### 6.1 Rights Summary

| Right | Description | Response Time |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Access** | Obtain confirmation of processing and a copy of your data | 30 days |
| **Rectification** | Correct inaccurate or incomplete data | 30 days |
| **Erasure** | Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten") | 30 days |
| **Restriction** | Limit how we process your data | 30 days |
| **Portability** | Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format | 30 days |
| **Objection** | Object to processing based on legitimate interest | 30 days |
| **Withdraw Consent** | Withdraw previously given consent at any time | Immediate |
| **Automated Decisions** | Not be subject to solely automated decisions with legal effect | 30 days |

### 6.2 Exercising Your Rights

**Request Submission:**
- Email: privacy@greenlang.io
- In-Platform: Settings > Privacy > Data Requests
- Mail: Data Protection Officer, GreenLang Inc., [Address]

**Verification:**
We verify your identity before processing requests by:
- Confirming account ownership via registered email
- Requesting additional verification for sensitive requests
- Using secure verification links

**Response Process:**
1. Request received and acknowledged within 3 business days
2. Identity verification completed
3. Request evaluated and processed
4. Response provided within 30 calendar days
5. Extension of up to 60 additional days for complex requests (with notification)

### 6.3 Right to Access (Article 15)

Upon verified request, we provide:
- Confirmation of whether we process your personal data
- Copy of personal data in a commonly used electronic format
- Information about purposes, categories, recipients, retention, and rights
- Source of data if not collected directly from you

### 6.4 Right to Erasure (Article 17)

You may request erasure when:
- Data is no longer necessary for original purpose
- You withdraw consent (where consent was the legal basis)
- You object to processing and no overriding legitimate grounds exist
- Data was unlawfully processed
- Erasure is required by law

**Exceptions:** We may retain data when required for:
- Legal compliance obligations
- Establishing, exercising, or defending legal claims
- Public interest in public health
- Archiving in the public interest

### 6.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

For data processed by automated means based on consent or contract, we provide:
- Data in structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (JSON, CSV)
- Direct transmission to another controller where technically feasible

## 12. Privacy Impact Assessments

### 12.1 When Required

Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs/DPIAs) are conducted when:
- Processing is likely to result in high risk to individuals
- Introducing new technologies with privacy implications
- Large-scale processing of sensitive data
- Systematic monitoring of public areas
- Automated decision-making with legal effects
- Processing children's data
- Cross-border data transfers to non-adequate countries

### 12.2 Assessment Process

1. **Description:** Document the processing activity
2. **Necessity:** Assess necessity and proportionality
3. **Risk Assessment:** Identify and assess risks to data subjects
4. **Mitigation:** Identify measures to mitigate risks
5. **Review:** DPO review and approval
6. **Consultation:** Supervisory authority consultation if high residual risk
7. **Documentation:** Maintain DPIA records

## 13. Data Breach Notification

### 13.1 Notification to Supervisory Authority

In the event of a personal data breach:

| Requirement | Timeline |
|-------------|----------|
| Internal escalation | Immediate upon discovery |
| Risk assessment | Within 24 hours |
| DPA notification (if required) | Within 72 hours |
| Documentation | Immediate and ongoing |

Notification to the supervisory authority includes:
- Nature of the breach
- Categories and approximate number of data subjects
- Categories and approximate number of records
- Contact details of DPO
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken or proposed

### 13.2 Notification to Data Subjects

When a breach is likely to result in high risk to individuals:

| Requirement | Timeline |
|-------------|----------|
| Risk assessment | Within 48 hours of discovery |
| Individual notification | Without undue delay |
| Method | Direct communication (email, letter) |

Notification to individuals includes:
- Clear description of the breach
- Contact details of DPO
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken to address the breach
- Recommendations for individuals to protect themselves

### 13.3 Exceptions to Individual Notification

Individual notification is not required when:
- Data was encrypted or otherwise unintelligible
- Measures taken ensure high risk is no longer likely
- Individual notification would require disproportionate effort (public communication may substitute)
