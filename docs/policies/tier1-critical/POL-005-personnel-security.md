## 1. Purpose

This Personnel Security Policy establishes requirements for managing security risks associated with the employment lifecycle, from pre-employment screening through termination. Personnel are both the greatest asset and a significant security consideration for any organization. This policy ensures that:

- Appropriate screening verifies trustworthiness before granting access
- Personnel understand their security responsibilities through training and acknowledgment
- Security is maintained during role changes and transfers
- Access is revoked promptly and completely upon termination
- Insider threat risks are identified and mitigated

This policy applies to all phases of the employment relationship to protect GreenLang, our customers, and our personnel.

---

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 Applicability

This policy applies to:

- **Employees**: Full-time, part-time, and temporary employees
- **Contractors**: Independent contractors and consulting personnel
- **Interns**: Paid and unpaid interns
- **Contingent Workers**: Agency workers, temporary staff
- **Board Members**: Directors requiring system access
- **Third-Party Personnel**: Vendor employees requiring GreenLang access

### 2.2 Lifecycle Phases Covered

- Pre-employment/Pre-engagement
- Onboarding
- Role changes and transfers
- Ongoing employment
- Termination (voluntary and involuntary)
- Post-employment obligations

---

## 3. Policy Statement

### 3.1 Core Principles

All personnel with access to GreenLang systems and data must:

- Undergo appropriate pre-employment screening proportionate to role sensitivity
- Complete security awareness training before receiving system access
- Acknowledge and comply with security policies
- Report security incidents and concerns
- Maintain confidentiality during and after employment
- Surrender access and assets upon termination

### 3.2 Trust and Verify

GreenLang extends trust to personnel based on verified screening, completed training, and signed agreements. This trust is maintained through ongoing awareness, monitoring, and periodic verification.

## 4. Pre-Employment Screening

### 4.1 Screening Requirements by Role Sensitivity

| Role Sensitivity | Description | Screening Components |
|-----------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Standard** | General business roles with Internal data access | Identity verification, right to work, criminal check (7 years), employment verification (3 years) |
| **Elevated** | Roles with Confidential data access or supervisory duties | Standard + education verification, professional reference checks (2), credit check (if financially sensitive) |
| **High** | Roles with Restricted data access, privileged system access, or security responsibilities | Elevated + global criminal check, extended employment verification (5 years), additional references |
| **Executive** | C-level, VP, and senior leadership | High + enhanced background investigation, board approval |

### 4.2 Role Sensitivity Classification

| Role Category | Sensitivity Level | Examples |
|--------------|-------------------|----------|
| Administrative | Standard | Office administration, facilities, general business |
| Engineering | Elevated | Software developers, data engineers, QA |
| Engineering (Production) | High | SRE, DevOps with production access, DBAs |
| Security | High | Security engineers, security analysts, SOC |
| Finance | Elevated/High | Accountants, financial analysts, payroll |
| Executive | Executive | C-suite, VPs, Directors |
| HR/Legal | Elevated | HR personnel, legal counsel |
| Customer-facing | Standard/Elevated | Support, sales, customer success |
| IT Operations | High | System administrators, network engineers |

### 4.3 Screening Components

**Identity Verification:**
- Government-issued photo ID verification
- Right to work documentation
- Address verification

**Criminal Background Check:**
- Federal and state criminal records (US)
- National criminal records (international)
- Sex offender registry check
- Sanctions and watch list screening (OFAC, etc.)

**Employment Verification:**
- Confirmation of previous employment
- Dates of employment
- Job title/role
- Reason for leaving (where obtainable)

**Education Verification:**
- Highest degree claimed
- Institution attended
- Graduation date
- Degree field

**Credit Check (where applicable):**
- Required for roles with financial responsibility
- Required for roles with access to financial systems
- Conducted with candidate consent
- Reviewed by HR, not hiring manager

**Reference Checks:**
- Professional references (not personal)
- Direct supervisors preferred
- Verification of work quality and ethics

### 4.4 International Considerations

For personnel based outside the United States:

- Screening conducted in compliance with local law
- Equivalent local screening services used
- Cultural and legal differences documented
- GDPR-compliant consent and processing

### 4.5 Screening Timeline

- Screening initiated upon conditional offer acceptance
- Results typically available within 5-10 business days
- Start date contingent on satisfactory screening completion
- Adverse findings reviewed by HR before adverse action

## 5. Screening Criteria and Disqualifying Factors

### 5.1 Adjudication Process

Background check results are reviewed by HR in consultation with Security when needed. Final employment decisions consider:

- Nature and gravity of the offense
- Time elapsed since offense
- Relevance to the position
- Evidence of rehabilitation
- Accuracy of candidate disclosure

### 5.2 Potentially Disqualifying Factors

The following may result in adverse employment decision:

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| Felony conviction | Reviewed case-by-case; violent crimes or fraud typically disqualifying |
| Identity fraud | Disqualifying |
| Falsified application/resume | Disqualifying |
| Failed drug screening | Per company drug policy; may be disqualifying |
| Sanctions list match | Disqualifying |
| Material credit issues | For financially sensitive roles; case-by-case |
| Failure to disclose | May be disqualifying if material |

### 5.3 Adverse Action Process

When adverse action is contemplated based on screening results:

1. Pre-adverse action notice provided to candidate
2. Copy of screening report provided
3. Summary of rights provided (FCRA)
4. Candidate has opportunity to dispute or explain (5 business days)
5. Final decision made after review
6. Final adverse action notice (if applicable)

## 6. Confidentiality and NDA Requirements

### 6.1 Confidentiality Agreement

All personnel must execute a Confidentiality and Intellectual Property Agreement before:

- Receiving access credentials
- Accessing company systems
- Beginning work on GreenLang projects

**Agreement Contents:**

- Definition of confidential information
- Non-disclosure obligations
- Intellectual property assignment
- Non-solicitation provisions (where enforceable)
- Survival of obligations post-employment
- Return of information obligations

### 6.2 Additional NDAs

Role-specific NDAs may be required for:

- Access to M&A information
- Customer-specific confidential information
- Security vulnerability details
- Partnership negotiations
- Product roadmap information

### 6.3 NDA Updates

NDAs are reviewed and updated:

- Upon role change to more sensitive position
- When accessing new categories of confidential information
- At contract renewal for contractors
- When legal or regulatory requirements change

## 7. Security Awareness Training

### 7.1 Training Requirements

| Training Type | Audience | Timing | Duration | Passing Score |
|---------------|----------|--------|----------|---------------|
| Security Awareness | All personnel | Within 30 days of start | 45-60 minutes | 80% |
| Security Awareness Refresher | All personnel | Annually | 30-45 minutes | 80% |
| Secure Coding | Developers | Within 30 days of start | 2-4 hours | 80% |
| Secure Coding Refresher | Developers | Annually | 1-2 hours | 80% |
| Data Handling | Data processors | Within 30 days of start | 1 hour | 80% |
| Incident Response | Responders | Within 30 days of role | 2 hours | 85% |
| Privileged User | Admins/privileged users | Within 14 days of access | 1-2 hours | 85% |
| Compliance (GDPR, etc.) | Relevant roles | Within 30 days | 1-2 hours | 80% |

### 7.2 Training Content

**Security Awareness Training covers:**

- Information security principles and importance
- Data classification and handling
- Password and authentication security
- Phishing and social engineering recognition
- Physical security and clean desk
- Incident reporting procedures
- Acceptable use overview
- Remote work security
- Mobile device security

**Role-Specific Training covers:**

- Technical controls relevant to role
- Regulatory requirements (GDPR, SOC 2, etc.)
- Secure development practices (for developers)
- Incident response procedures (for responders)
- Privileged access responsibilities (for admins)

### 7.3 Training Completion Tracking

- Training completion tracked in LMS
- Automated reminders sent for upcoming/overdue training
- Manager notification for overdue training
- Access may be restricted for significantly overdue training
- Completion records retained for audit purposes

### 7.4 Failure to Complete Training

| Overdue Period | Action |
|----------------|--------|
| 1-14 days | Automated reminder emails |
| 15-30 days | Manager notification; escalation |
| 31-45 days | Director notification; final warning |
| 46+ days | Access suspension until completion; HR review |

## 8. Acceptable Use Acknowledgment

### 8.1 Acknowledgment Requirement

All personnel must acknowledge the Acceptable Use Policy (POL-006) before:

- Receiving system access credentials
- Using company-provided equipment
- Accessing company networks or data

### 8.2 Acknowledgment Process

1. Policy presented during onboarding orientation
2. Electronic acknowledgment captured in HR system
3. Acknowledgment recorded with date/time stamp
4. Copy provided to employee for reference
5. Re-acknowledgment required annually or upon policy update

### 8.3 Required Acknowledgments

| Confidentiality Agreement | Before start | N/A (one-time) |
| Data Classification Policy | Before access | Annual |
| Remote Work Agreement | Before remote work | Annual |
| BYOD Agreement | Before BYOD use | Annual |

## 9. Ongoing Personnel Assessment

### 9.1 Continuous Monitoring

GreenLang employs the following continuous monitoring controls:

| Control | Purpose | Scope |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Access logging | Audit trail of system access | All personnel |
| User behavior analytics (UEBA) | Anomaly detection for insider threat | All personnel |
| Data loss prevention (DLP) | Detection of unauthorized data movement | All personnel |
| Badge access logs | Physical access monitoring | All personnel |
| Phishing simulation | Security awareness measurement | All personnel |
| Privileged access monitoring | Enhanced oversight of elevated access | Privileged users |

### 9.2 Periodic Verification

| Activity | Frequency | Audience | Purpose |
|----------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Access review | Quarterly (privileged), Semi-annual (standard) | All personnel | Verify appropriate access |
| Training completion | Annual | All personnel | Ensure current knowledge |
| Policy acknowledgment | Annual | All personnel | Confirm awareness |
| Credential hygiene | Continuous | All personnel | Password security |
| Security clearance renewal | Per requirements | Cleared personnel | Maintain clearance |

### 9.3 Performance and Conduct

Security-relevant conduct is considered in performance evaluation:

- Compliance with security policies
- Timely completion of training
- Incident reporting responsiveness
- Handling of confidential information
- Physical security adherence

## 10. Role Change Procedures

### 10.1 Internal Transfers and Promotions

When personnel change roles within GreenLang:

**Access Review:**

1. New role access requirements documented
2. Current access compared to new requirements
3. Access no longer needed is revoked
4. New access provisioned per normal process
5. Access changes completed within 5 business days

**Training Assessment:**

1. New role training requirements identified
2. Gap analysis performed
3. Additional training assigned
4. Training completed within 30 days
5. Access to sensitive functions contingent on training

**Documentation Updates:**

1. HR records updated
2. Access management system updated
3. Reporting structure updated
4. Emergency contact information verified

### 10.2 Sensitivity Level Changes

When role change involves different sensitivity level:

| Change | Requirements |
|--------|--------------|
| Standard to Elevated | Enhanced screening if not previously completed |
| Elevated to High | Additional background check components; privileged user training |
| Any to Executive | Board notification; enhanced background investigation |
| Higher to Lower | Access reduction; no additional screening |

### 10.3 NDA Updates

Role changes may require:

- Acknowledgment of access to new categories of information
- Additional NDA for specific projects or access
- Update to data handling responsibilities

## 11. Termination and Exit Procedures

### 11.1 Termination Types

| Type | Description | Notice Period | Access Revocation |
|------|-------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Voluntary - Standard | Resignation with notice | Per policy (2 weeks typical) | Last day of employment |
| Voluntary - Immediate | Resignation effective immediately | None | Same day |
| Involuntary - Performance | Termination for performance | Per HR policy | Last day of employment |
| Involuntary - Cause | Termination for misconduct | None | Immediate |
| Involuntary - Reduction | Layoff or position elimination | Per WARN requirements | Last day of employment |
| Contractor - End of Contract | Scheduled contract completion | Per contract | Contract end date |
| Contractor - Early Termination | Contract terminated early | Per contract | Termination date |
| Death | Deceased employee | N/A | Same day of notification |

### 11.2 Exit Interview

Exit interviews are conducted for all departing personnel (except involuntary for cause):

**Interview Components:**

- Reminder of confidentiality obligations
- NDA reminder and acknowledgment
- Return of assets checklist
- Knowledge transfer status
- Outstanding obligations
- Final access revocation notification
- Contact information for future reference

**Security-Specific Topics:**

- Reminder not to retain company data
- Verification of no personal devices with company data
- Confirmation of cloud account disconnection
- Reminder of intellectual property obligations
- Reporting obligations for future security concerns

### 11.3 Access Revocation Timeline

| Termination Type | Access Revocation SLA | Priority |
|-----------------|----------------------|----------|
| Involuntary - Cause | Immediate (within 15 minutes) | P0 |
| Involuntary - Other | Within 4 hours of last day | P1 |
| Voluntary - Immediate | Same day | P1 |
| Voluntary - Standard | End of last business day | P2 |
| Contractor End | End of contract day | P2 |

### 11.4 Credential Revocation Checklist

**Immediate Actions (all terminations):**

- [ ] Active Directory / SSO account disabled
- [ ] VPN access revoked
- [ ] Email access disabled
- [ ] MFA devices deregistered
- [ ] Password changed on any shared/service accounts known to user

**Same-Day Actions:**

- [ ] Cloud console access revoked (AWS, GCP, Azure)
- [ ] Source code repository access revoked
- [ ] Database access revoked
- [ ] API keys and tokens rotated/revoked
- [ ] SSH keys removed from authorized_keys
- [ ] Badge access disabled
- [ ] Building access revoked
- [ ] Parking access revoked

**Follow-Up Actions (within 24 hours):**

- [ ] Distribution list removal
- [ ] Shared mailbox access removed
- [ ] Collaboration tool access (Slack, Teams) revoked
- [ ] SaaS application access revoked
- [ ] Ticketing system access revoked
- [ ] Documentation platform access revoked
- [ ] Calendar delegations removed
- [ ] Manager notified of completion

### 11.5 Equipment Return

| Item | Collection Method | Deadline |
|------|------------------|----------|
| Laptop | In-person (preferred) or shipped | Last day or within 5 days |
| Mobile devices | In-person or shipped | Last day or within 5 days |
| Security tokens | In-person | Last day |
| Access badges | In-person | Last day |
| Keys | In-person | Last day |
| Company credit cards | In-person or mail | Last day |
| Printed materials | In-person or secure destruction | Last day |

**Non-Return Consequences:**

- Final paycheck may be delayed (where legal)
- Charges deducted from final pay (where legal)
- Legal action for unreturned equipment
- Report to law enforcement if warranted

## 12. Post-Employment Obligations

### 12.1 Continuing Obligations

After employment ends, personnel remain bound by:

- Confidentiality obligations (indefinite for trade secrets)
- Non-disclosure agreements
- Intellectual property assignments
- Non-solicitation provisions (if applicable and enforceable)
- Non-compete provisions (if applicable and enforceable)
- Legal hold obligations (if notified)

### 12.2 Reference Requests

- Employment verification limited to dates and title
- Additional information requires signed release
- Security-relevant concerns documented (not shared externally)

### 12.3 Re-Hire Considerations

Former personnel seeking re-employment:

- New background check required if >12 months since departure
- Previous access and conduct history reviewed
- New onboarding and training required
- Previous access levels not automatically restored
