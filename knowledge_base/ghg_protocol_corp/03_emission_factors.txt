GREENHOUSE GAS EMISSION FACTORS: REFERENCE DATABASE
=============================================================================

1. PURPOSE AND USAGE

This document provides reference emission factors for common fuels, electricity grids, and activities. These factors enable calculation of GHG emissions using the basic formula:

GHG Emissions = Activity Data × Emission Factor

Important Notes:
- Use the most specific emission factor available (site > region > national > default)
- Document the source and vintage of all emission factors used
- Update factors when new authoritative data becomes available
- All factors in this document are from recognized authorities (EPA, DEFRA, IEA, IPCC)

2. STATIONARY COMBUSTION EMISSION FACTORS
=============================================================================

2.1 NATURAL GAS

Natural Gas Combustion (kg CO2 per unit):
- Per therm: 5.3 kg CO2
- Per million BTU (MMBtu): 53.06 kg CO2
- Per cubic meter: 1.91 kg CO2
- Per thousand cubic feet (MCF): 54.00 kg CO2

Full GHG Emissions (kg CO2e per MMBtu):
- CO2: 53.06 kg
- CH4: 0.001 kg × GWP 29.8 = 0.030 kg CO2e
- N2O: 0.0001 kg × GWP 273 = 0.027 kg CO2e
- Total: 53.12 kg CO2e per MMBtu

Source: US EPA Greenhouse Gas Emission Factors (2024)

Regional Variations:
- US natural gas: 53.06 kg CO2/MMBtu (standard)
- European natural gas: 56.1 kg CO2/MMBtu (higher density)

2.2 FUEL OIL AND DIESEL

Diesel Fuel / Distillate Fuel Oil No. 2:

Residual Fuel Oil No. 6:
- Per gallon: 11.27 kg CO2
- Per liter: 2.98 kg CO2

Source: US EPA

Sub-bituminous Coal:

2.4 PROPANE AND LPG

Propane (Liquefied Petroleum Gas):
- Per gallon: 5.68 kg CO2

2.5 BIOMASS FUELS

Wood and Wood Residuals:
- Per short ton: 1,685 kg biogenic CO2 (reported separately, not in Scope 1)
- Per MMBtu: 93.80 kg biogenic CO2

Note: Biogenic CO2 from biomass combustion is not counted in Scope 1, 2, or 3 emissions but should be reported separately. Include only fossil-derived CH4 and N2O emissions in Scope 1.

3. MOBILE COMBUSTION EMISSION FACTORS
=============================================================================

3.1 GASOLINE (MOTOR VEHICLES)

Gasoline Combustion:
- Per gallon: 8.89 kg CO2
- Per liter: 2.35 kg CO2

Typical Vehicle Fuel Economy and Annual Emissions:

Passenger Car (25 MPG average, 12,000 miles/year):
- Fuel consumed: 12,000 / 25 = 480 gallons
- Emissions: 480 gallons × 8.89 kg/gallon = 4,267 kg = 4.27 tons CO2/year

Light Truck/SUV (18 MPG average, 12,000 miles/year):
- Fuel consumed: 12,000 / 18 = 667 gallons
- Emissions: 667 gallons × 8.89 kg/gallon = 5,930 kg = 5.93 tons CO2/year

Source: US EPA

3.2 DIESEL (MOBILE)

Diesel for Highway Vehicles:
- Per gallon: 10.21 kg CO2
- Per liter: 2.70 kg CO2

Heavy-Duty Truck (6 MPG average, 50,000 miles/year):
- Fuel consumed: 50,000 / 6 = 8,333 gallons
- Emissions: 8,333 gallons × 10.21 kg/gallon = 85,080 kg = 85.08 tons CO2/year

3.3 AVIATION FUEL

Jet Fuel (Aviation Gasoline):
- Per gallon: 9.57 kg CO2
- Per liter: 2.53 kg CO2

Commercial Aviation (passenger-mile basis):
- Short-haul (<300 miles): 0.25 kg CO2e per passenger-mile
- Medium-haul (300-2,300 miles): 0.18 kg CO2e per passenger-mile
- Long-haul (>2,300 miles): 0.15 kg CO2e per passenger-mile

Example Flight:
New York to Los Angeles: 2,500 miles, long-haul
Emissions per passenger = 2,500 miles × 0.15 kg/mile = 375 kg = 0.375 tons CO2e

Source: DEFRA 2024 Conversion Factors

3.4 MARINE FUEL

- Per gallon: 11.27 kg CO2
- Per liter: 2.98 kg CO2

Marine Diesel Oil:
- Per gallon: 10.21 kg CO2

5. FUGITIVE EMISSIONS FACTORS
=============================================================================

5.1 REFRIGERANTS (HIGH GWP)

Global Warming Potential (GWP) 100-year (IPCC AR6):

HFCs (Hydrofluorocarbons):
- R-134a (common automotive, commercial refrigeration): GWP 1,530
- R-410A (residential AC): GWP 2,256
- R-404A (commercial refrigeration): GWP 4,271
- R-32 (newer AC systems): GWP 771

CFCs (Chlorofluorocarbons - phased out):
- R-12: GWP 10,900 (ozone-depleting, banned)

Natural Refrigerants (low GWP alternatives):
- R-290 (Propane): GWP 3.3
- R-600a (Isobutane): GWP 3.3
- R-744 (CO2): GWP 1
- Ammonia (R-717): GWP <1

Calculation Example:
HVAC system with R-134a, annual leakage 15 kg
GWP = 1,530
Emissions = 15 kg × 1,530 / 1,000 = 22.95 tons CO2e

Typical Annual Leakage Rates:
- Well-maintained systems: 2-5% of charge
- Average systems: 10-15% of charge
- Poorly maintained: 20-30% of charge

5.2 METHANE FROM NATURAL GAS LEAKAGE

Methane (CH4) from natural gas systems:
- GWP of methane (IPCC AR6): 29.8 (100-year)
- Typical leakage rate: 0.5-2% of throughput

Example:
Natural gas consumption: 100,000 MMBtu/year
Assumed leakage: 1%
Methane in natural gas: ~95% by volume
Leakage: 100,000 MMBtu × 0.01 × 0.05 kg CH4/MMBtu = 50 kg CH4
Emissions = 50 kg × 29.8 = 1,490 kg = 1.49 tons CO2e

5.3 SULFUR HEXAFLUORIDE (SF6)

SF6 from electrical equipment:
- GWP (IPCC AR6): 25,200
- Typical annual leakage: 1-3% of charge

Example:
SF6-insulated switchgear, 50 kg charge, 2% annual leakage
Leakage: 50 kg × 0.02 = 1 kg
Emissions = 1 kg × 25,200 / 1,000 = 25.2 tons CO2e

6.1 PURCHASED GOODS (SPEND-BASED)

US EPA EEIO (Environmentally-Extended Input-Output) Factors (2024):
(tons CO2e per $1,000 spent)

Materials:
- Paper and paperboard: 0.89 tons CO2e/$1,000
- Plastics: 1.24 tons CO2e/$1,000
- Steel: 1.56 tons CO2e/$1,000
- Aluminum: 2.03 tons CO2e/$1,000
- Cement: 0.94 tons CO2e/$1,000
- Chemicals: 1.12 tons CO2e/$1,000

Services:
- IT services: 0.21 tons CO2e/$1,000
- Consulting services: 0.15 tons CO2e/$1,000
- Facilities services: 0.34 tons CO2e/$1,000

Food Products:
- Meat products: 1.45 tons CO2e/$1,000
- Dairy products: 1.02 tons CO2e/$1,000
- Grain products: 0.68 tons CO2e/$1,000

Example:
Annual spend on steel: $500,000
Emission factor: 1.56 tons CO2e/$1,000
Emissions = $500,000 / $1,000 × 1.56 = 500 × 1.56 = 780 tons CO2e

6.2 TRANSPORTATION (DISTANCE-BASED)

Freight Transport (kg CO2 per ton-mile):
- Heavy-duty truck: 0.062 kg CO2 per ton-mile
- Rail: 0.021 kg CO2 per ton-mile
- Ocean shipping: 0.012 kg CO2 per ton-mile
- Air freight: 0.602 kg CO2 per ton-mile

Example:
Ship 100 tons of goods 1,000 miles by truck
Emissions = 100 tons × 1,000 miles × 0.062 kg/ton-mile = 6,200 kg = 6.2 tons CO2

Business Travel:
- Rental car (per mile): 0.350 kg CO2e
- Hotel (per night): 12.1 kg CO2e per room-night
- Rail (per passenger-mile): 0.041 kg CO2e

Source: DEFRA 2024 Conversion Factors

Waste Treatment (kg CO2e per ton):
- Landfill (mixed waste): 500 kg CO2e per ton (includes methane from decomposition)
- Incineration: 420 kg CO2e per ton
- Recycling (avoided emissions):
  * Paper recycling: -750 kg CO2e per ton (net negative)

Source: US EPA WARM Model (Waste Reduction Model)

7. TRANSMISSION & DISTRIBUTION LOSSES (SCOPE 3 CATEGORY 3)
=============================================================================

Electricity T&D Loss Factors:

US Average: 5.0% of purchased electricity
- Apply grid emission factor to 5% of purchased electricity for T&D loss emissions

Example:
Purchased electricity: 1,000 MWh
Grid factor: 0.417 tons CO2/MWh
T&D loss: 1,000 MWh × 5% = 50 MWh
T&D emissions = 50 MWh × 0.417 = 20.85 tons CO2e

International T&D Losses (% of electricity):
- EU average: 6%
- India: 20% (high technical + commercial losses)
- Brazil: 15%
- China: 5.5%

Source: IEA World Energy Statistics

8. UNCERTAINTY AND DATA QUALITY
=============================================================================

Emission Factor Uncertainty Ranges (typical):

Tier 1 (Site-specific, continuous measurement): ±2-5%
Tier 2 (Regional/supplier-specific factors): ±10-15%
Tier 3 (National averages): ±15-30%
Tier 4 (Spend-based EEIO factors): ±50-100%

Best Practices:
1. Document all emission factor sources and vintages
2. Use most recent factors available
3. Apply consistent factors year-over-year for comparability
4. Update factors when new authoritative data published
5. Conduct sensitivity analysis for material sources
6. Prioritize improving factors for largest emission sources

=============================================================================
9. UPDATING EMISSION FACTORS
=============================================================================

Recommended Update Frequency:

Electricity Grid Factors:
- Update annually when new eGRID or IEA data published
- eGRID: Released ~2 years after data year (2023 data published 2024)

Fuel Factors:
- Update every 2-3 years or when IPCC assessment reports published
- Fuel CO2 factors relatively stable
- GWP values change with IPCC assessment cycles

Spend-Based Factors:
- Update every 2-3 years when EPA EEIO updated

Regional/Supplier Factors:
- Use most current data available
- Request annual updates from suppliers
