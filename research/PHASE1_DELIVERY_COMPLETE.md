## Key Insights & Recommendations

### Critical Findings

1. **Data Center Efficiency Matters:**
   - Legacy facilities (PUE 1.8) emit 64% more than hyperscale (PUE 1.1)
   - Cloud region selection can reduce emissions by 10× (France 0.052 vs. India 0.710 kg CO2e/kWh)
   - **Recommendation:** Migrate to hyperscale cloud providers in low-carbon regions

2. **"Green" Hydrogen Requires Truly Green Electricity:**
   - Grid-based electrolysis in coal-heavy grids (China, India, Germany) emits MORE than grey hydrogen
   - Only grids <0.2 kg CO2e/kWh achieve GHG reduction (France, Brazil, California)
   - **Recommendation:** Use dedicated renewable electricity or blue hydrogen until grid decarbonizes

3. **SAF Waste-Based Pathways Are Best:**
   - MSW (11.8 g/MJ) and UCO (13.5 g/MJ) achieve 85-87% reduction
   - PtL e-kerosene (8.5 g/MJ) achieves 90% but costs 5-10× more
   - **Recommendation:** Prioritize HEFA from waste oils and FT from MSW for near-term SAF adoption

4. **Recycling Delivers Massive Impact:**
   - Aluminum recycling avoids 95% of virgin emissions (11.9 kg CO2e/kg)
   - Plastic recycling avoids 70-75% (1.4-2.3 kg CO2e/kg)
   - **Recommendation:** Maximize recycled content procurement, track avoided emissions

5. **Carbon Removal Has Wide Cost Range:**
   - Afforestation: $5-30/tonne CO2 (lowest cost)
   - Biochar: $150-300/tonne CO2
   - DAC: $600-1,000/tonne CO2 (highest cost, highest permanence)
   - **Recommendation:** Portfolio approach - combine nature-based and technological CDR
