### The Problem: 140 Engineers in 12 Months is Impossible

**Reality Check:**
- Hiring 12 engineers per month for 12 months is unprecedented
- Cost: $30M in Year 1 salaries alone ($200K average Ã— 150 engineers)
- Risk: Quality suffers with rushed hiring
- Management overhead: Impossible to onboard and manage
- Culture: Company culture deteriorates with hyper-growth

**Solution:** Build agents to act as "virtual employees" doing repetitive work

### Agent-as-Employee Design Principles

**1. Agents Should Be Specialists, Not Generalists**
- Each agent has a specific job function (like a human employee)
- Clear responsibilities, inputs, outputs
- Measurable performance metrics

**2. Agents Should Collaborate Like Teams**
- Multi-agent workflows (agent orchestration)
- Handoffs between agents (like departments)
- Escalation paths (when agent can't handle â†’ human intervention)

**3. Agents Should Improve Over Time**
- Feedback loops (learn from corrections)
- A/B testing (try new prompts, compare results)
- Version upgrades (like employee training)

**4. Agents Should Have Clear "Management"**
- Agent performance dashboards
- SLAs (success rate, response time)
- Cost tracking (LLM tokens used)
